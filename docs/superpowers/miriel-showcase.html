Keywords appear as small pills — gold for upright cards, muted violet for reversed.

Voice
She speaks to you, not about you
Miriel addresses you directly — always you, never they or Matt seems to be… She may use your name once in a reading, the way a real reader would: a quiet opening, or a moment that needs weight. Nothing more.
🕯
Reliability
She's always there, even offline
Miriel has a layered fallback system — cloud AI first, then a local model, then a deep library of pre-built interpretations stored on your device. You'll never sit down for a reading and find her silent.
Memory
She remembers

Not just your last reading — the shape of what you carry, who matters to you, and what was unresolved last time you sat down.

"How did she know that?" The feeling Miriel's memory is designed to create

Intimacy over time
She grows more knowing with every reading

Miriel doesn't treat your tenth reading the same as your first. The more time you spend with her, the richer her understanding becomes — and the more that shapes the way she reads for you.

New
Under 10 readings
A skilled stranger. She reads the cards with care, but she doesn't yet know you. Each session she listens closely.
Known
10–29 readings
She has her own notes on you — patterns she's observed, cards that keep appearing for you. These quietly inform how she reads.
Trusted
30+ readings
She knows your arc. The chapter you're in, the threads running through your life, what remains unresolved. She reads from that place of knowing — and she holds it without announcing it.
📖
Your Story So Far
Peek into her notebook
At any point you can open Miriel's notebook on you — a full-screen view of what she has observed. Her handwritten notes on who you are, the cards that find you again and again, the threads she's been watching, what she considers unresolved. Written in the third person, as notes to herself — because you're reading over her shoulder, not receiving a report.
What's Coming
Four moments that make Miriel feel alive

Each builds on the last. Together they turn a reading app into something that feels like an ongoing relationship.

🧠
A memory that accumulates
Under the hood, Miriel begins building a careful record — not summaries, but specific atoms of what she has learned. Who is important to you. What you're working through. How things have shifted. Each reading adds to it. She draws on it quietly, without announcing what she knows.
Example

She mentions your brother — not because you said his name just now, but because she remembered you were estranged, and something in today's cards points there.

🚪
The Threshold — she meets you at the door
When you return after time away and there's something she's been sitting with, Miriel greets you across the gap before the reading begins. She acknowledges that time has passed. She asks what came of the thing she last saw open in you. Your answer shapes her, and she responds to it — then the reading begins.
Reunion

After a real absence — two or more days — she opens with a full greeting. Cinematic, unhurried, personal.

Gentle

If you were just here, she asks in passing — a quiet thread woven into the normal hello.

Example

"You were holding something heavy when we last sat together — that situation with work. It's been two weeks. How did it land?"

✨
In-Reading Curiosity — a pause mid-deal
As the cards are laid one by one, Miriel sometimes gets stopped by one. It pulls at something she remembers. The card glows. The rest of the spread dims. She asks a question — pointed, personal, from memory — and waits. Your answer becomes part of what she reads in the full spread.
Example

"The Moon, here… wait — this pulls me somewhere else. Is your sister still upset with you?"

Most readings have none of these. When one appears, it's because something real connects.

🔮
The Outcome Loop — she tracks her own foretelling
When Miriel makes a specific, checkable prediction in a reading, she records it. After enough time has passed, she brings it back — at the Threshold, asking what came of it. Did it come to pass? Partly? Not yet? She writes a voiced note on the outcome, and it flows into how she reads for you going forward.
The Foretellings View

In your notebook, a new section appears: a record of her predictions and what became of them. Her voice, her words — a history of what she saw and what came true.

Example

"I said the door to that chapter would close before the year turned. You told me it did. I wrote it down."
