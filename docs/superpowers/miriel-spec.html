Interpretation Cache & Reader Profile
Shipped May 25, 2026

Two systems working together: an offline fallback so Miriel never goes silent, and a graduated model of who you are to her.

Interpretation Cache
  • SQLite-backed third fallback tier: Claude → Ollama → Cache
  • Keyed by deck, spread type, position, card, and orientation — assembled card-by-card when no exact spread match exists
  • Claude entries always win over Ollama; Ollama only writes when no entry exists
  • Seeded via scripts/seed-cache.js --deck tarot before shipping
Graduated Oracle Model

Miriel builds a compact per-reader profile distilled from full history — never sending raw history during a live reading.

Auto-refreshes every 5 readings at Tier 2, every 10 at Tier 3.

Intimacy Tiers
TierThresholdWhat Miriel knows
1 Under 10 readings Nothing — baseline behaviour, no profile injection
2 10–29 readings miriel_notes and recurring cards
3 30+ readings Full arc: life_arc.current_chapter, key threads, unresolved thread, inflection points — plus the persona addition: "You have known this person through many readings… Read accordingly."

Second-Person Addressing
Shipped June 10, 2026

A canonical rule that stops Miriel narrating you in the third person and makes her speak to you directly — always.

"The person sitting across from you is {name}. They are right there — speak to them as 'you,' always. Never describe them in the third person or repeat their name back to them as if reading from a file. You may use their name at most once in a reading, only where a real reader would: a quiet greeting, or a single moment that needs weight."

What this fixed
  • Miriel was narrating readers in the third person ("Matt seems troubled") instead of speaking to them
  • Tier 2/3 readers had the name instruction injected twice (double-injection bug)
Applied at
  • POST /api/interpret
  • POST /api/clarify
  • POST /api/compatibility
  • POST /api/session-summary

Compatibility readings get a variant that addresses one person as "you" and names the other.

Memory Engine
Approved Phase 1 — Foundation June 13, 2026

The infrastructure underneath everything: atoms of what Miriel has learned, written to durable storage and recalled precisely when relevant.

Emotional target: "How did she know that?" plus "I want to come back and tell her what happened."

Four Layers
Capture
After each reading, a Haiku call extracts memory atoms as ADD / UPDATE / TOUCH operations — not a re-summarisation.
Store
data/memory.db — SQLite, separate from interpretations.db. Personal and precious; both are gitignored.
Recall
At POST /api/interpret, a deterministic scorer selects the top 3–5 relevant atoms and injects a "hold them lightly" block. No LLM on the critical path.
Synthesis
Your Story So Far notebook (prose profile) coexists with the atom layer — ambient sense vs. precise detail.
Memory Types
person thread event feeling prediction fact preference
Statuses
open moving resolved dormant
Recall Score Formula
score = 2.0 × salience_normalised + 1.5 × (open:1 | moving:0.6 | else:0) + 1.5 × keyword_overlap + 0.5 × recency_decay - 0.4 × overexposure
Anti-Hallucination Rules
  • Record only what is explicitly present in the reading
  • Inferences allowed but marked low salience (1–2) and phrased tentatively
  • If parsing fails, apply nothing
  • Back-fill migrates existing history through the same extractor, guarded by a memory_meta flag so it runs exactly once per reader
2
The Threshold
Approved Phase 2 — Reunion June 14, 2026

When you return, Miriel meets you across the gap — she remembers what was open and asks what came of it, before you say anything.

Emotional target: "A greeting you'd get visiting someone well known."

Reunion
Gap ≥ 2 days + open thread
Cinematic full-screen takeover. Miriel speaks across the gap, surfaces threads, asks what came of them.
Gentle
Gap < 2 days + open thread
Inline ask woven into the normal greeting. Same warmth, no ceremony.
None
No qualifying threads
Today's greeting, unchanged. No fabricated reunion.
The Two-Beat Exchange
  • Miriel speaks — generated greeting acknowledging the gap, surfacing threads, asking what came of them
  • You answer (free text) or skip
  • She responds only if answered — a voiced reflection, simultaneously captured to memory
  • Overlay dismisses into normal reading flow
Model Choice

Sonnet for greeting and reply — quality matters; latency is hidden by animation.

Haiku for answer capture — fast and lightweight, out of the critical path.

LLM failure → mode:'none' — never a wooden template reunion.

Qualifying Thread

Type thread, status open or moving, not previously asked (asked_at IS NULL), salience ≥ 3. Threads are marked asked at show time — not answer time — so she never re-asks the same one even if the user skips.

In-Reading Curiosity
Approved Phase 3 — Presence June 14, 2026

As cards are laid one by one, Miriel sometimes gets stopped by a card. It pulls at something she remembers. She pauses. She asks.

"The Moon, here… wait — this pulls me somewhere else. Is your sister still upset with you?"

The Paced Deal
  • ~2s between cards with ±250ms jitter; soft 14s total cap
  • CSS-scheduled animation replaced with a JS async deal loop (dealAndReveal())
  • Loop is interruptible at trigger card indices
The Pause
  • Trigger card glows and lifts; rest of spread dims to ~55%
  • A thread of light connects to a centred question panel
  • 3–4s minimum visible beat even on instant response
  • Deal resumes after answer or skip; remaining cards continue
Frequency & Discipline
  • 0–2 questions per reading — most readings have none
  • Only open or moving threads with salience ≥ 3 are candidates
  • Bias toward striking or less-obvious connections
  • Detection runs up front during the shuffle beat using Haiku — not during the deal
  • Answers fold into the interpretation as added context; same asked_at gate as the Threshold — skip still marks asked
4
Outcome Loop — Foretellings
Approved Phase 4 — Accountability June 14, 2026

Miriel is accountable to her own foretelling. She records specific predictions, asks about ripe ones at the Threshold, and writes a voiced outcome note that flows back into recall.

No tarot app tracks its own predictions.

Four Stages
StageWhat happensWhen
Capture When Miriel's synopsis contains a specific, checkable foretelling, it's recorded as type: prediction, status: open, salience ≥ 3. Vague encouragement is not a prediction. Folds into the existing capture pass — no extra LLM call. After each reading
Ripeness gate A prediction ripens after 14 + (id % 7) - 3 days — 11–17 day range with per-prediction jitter. Deterministic; never flickers. Uses asked_at so too_soon deferrals re-ripen correctly. On Threshold check
Follow-up Ripe predictions ride into the existing Threshold reunion alongside threads — one unified looking-back moment. Verdict choices: came_to_pass | did_not | partly | too_soon. At the Threshold
Voiced record A voiced outcome note in Miriel's voice ("The time with Maggie has ended.") stored as a linked event atom. Flows through recall() into future readings. Visible in a new Foretellings section in the notebook. After verdict
